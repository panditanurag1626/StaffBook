Job scams are becoming more common, especially targeting people who are actively looking for opportunities. While our platform onboards only verified employers, scammers can still find ways to mislead job seekers. It’s easy to get excited about a promising offer, but taking a moment to pause and verify can save you from serious trouble. Staying cautious helps protect both your money and your personal information, making your job search safer and more stress-free.

Red Flags of a Fake Job Offer

  • Requests for money — especially “refundable” fees.
  • Asking for sensitive details — such as credit card info, OTPs, Aadhaar, PAN, etc.
  • Poorly written job ads — spelling mistakes or unprofessional language.
  • Too-easy hiring process — instant offers with little or no interview.
  • Unrealistic salary promises — pay that seems too good to be true.
  • Suspicious email IDs or domains — e.g., unofficial or strange job portal addresses.
  • No clear company details — missing website, address, or proper contact information.
  • Pressure to act quickly — “limited time offer” or urgency to force quick decisions.
  • No proper job description — vague roles, unclear responsibilities, or unrealistic expectations.
  • Upfront document submission — asking for full documents before any formal interview or offer.

Protect Yourself from Fake Job Offers

  • Never share personal or financial information with unknown sources.
  • Do not pay any fees for job applications or offers.
  • Check the company’s online presence, reviews, and credibility before engaging.
  • Always research & verify the company thoroughly before applying or responding to any job offer.
  • Verify recruiter details, including email domain and contact information.
  • Take your time to evaluate offers—avoid rushing into decisions under pressure.
  • Be cautious of unsolicited job offers received via calls, messages, or emails.
  • Report any suspected job scams to the platform or relevant authorities.
